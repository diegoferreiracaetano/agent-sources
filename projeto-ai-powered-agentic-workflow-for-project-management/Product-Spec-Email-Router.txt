Product: Email Router

Problem Statement:
InnovateNext receives a growing volume of customer and internal operational email. Messages are manually triaged by support coordinators, causing slow response times, inconsistent routing, and missed high-priority issues.

Target Users:
- Support coordinators who need faster triage.
- Technical project managers who need visibility into routing logic and operational bottlenecks.
- Customer support agents who need messages assigned to the correct queue.

Goals:
- Classify incoming emails by intent, urgency, customer tier, and required team.
- Route each email to the correct queue with an explanation.
- Escalate urgent or enterprise customer messages.
- Provide an audit log of routing decisions.
- Allow administrators to update routing rules without code changes.

Non-Goals:
- The pilot will not send outbound email responses automatically.
- The pilot will not replace the existing ticketing system.

Core Requirements:
1. Ingest email metadata and body text from a mailbox or ticketing connector.
2. Detect categories such as billing, technical support, account access, product feedback, and sales inquiry.
3. Detect urgency signals such as outage, blocked user, security concern, refund request, or enterprise customer.
4. Route emails to queues: Support Tier 1, Support Tier 2, Billing, Sales, Security, Product Feedback, or Escalations.
5. Attach routing explanation, confidence score, and rule or model version.
6. Store routing decision history for audit and analytics.
7. Provide an admin configuration file or UI for queue rules and escalation thresholds.

Success Metrics:
- Reduce average manual triage time by 50%.
- Achieve at least 85% correct initial routing in pilot review.
- Ensure 100% of urgent enterprise messages receive escalation metadata.
- Provide audit history for every routed message.

Constraints:
- Must integrate with the existing ticketing workflow.
- Must protect customer data and avoid exposing sensitive content in logs.
- Must support a human override for incorrect routing.
- Must be observable through metrics and routing error reports.
